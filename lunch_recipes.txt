Recipe Name: Egg Fried Rice
Meal Type: Lunch
Cuisine: Chinese
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: cooked rice (2 cups), eggs (2), onion (1), carrot (1 small), capsicum (1/2), garlic (3 cloves), soy sauce (2 tbsp), pepper, salt, oil, spring onion
Steps:
1. Use leftover or day-old rice for best results.
2. Heat oil in a wok or large pan on high flame.
3. Add minced garlic and sauté for 30 seconds.
4. Add chopped onion, carrot, and capsicum. Stir fry for 2-3 minutes.
5. Push veggies to the side. Crack eggs in the center and scramble them.
6. Mix eggs and veggies together.
7. Add rice and mix everything well on high flame.
8. Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Toss well.
9. Garnish with spring onion and serve hot.
Notes: Use high flame throughout for authentic fried rice flavor. Don't use freshly cooked warm rice.

Recipe Name: Tomato Rice
Meal Type: Lunch
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Medium
Ingredients: cooked rice (2 cups), tomatoes (3 medium), onion (1), mustard seeds, curry leaves, dry red chilli (2), garlic (2 cloves), turmeric (1/4 tsp), chilli powder (1/2 tsp), salt, oil, coriander
Steps:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let splutter.
2. Add dry red chillies and curry leaves.
3. Add chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until golden.
4. Add chopped tomatoes. Cook until mushy and oil separates.
5. Add turmeric, chilli powder, and salt. Mix.
6. Add cooked rice and mix gently. Heat for 2 minutes.
7. Garnish with coriander. Serve with curd or papad.
Notes: Add a dash of lemon juice at end for extra tang. Can be made spicier to taste.

Recipe Name: Lemon Rice
Meal Type: Lunch
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 15 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: cooked rice (2 cups), lemon (2), mustard seeds, curry leaves, peanuts (handful), green chilli (1), turmeric (1/4 tsp), salt, oil, coriander, urad dal (1 tsp)
Steps:
1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let splutter.
2. Add urad dal and roast until golden.
3. Add peanuts and roast for 1 minute.
4. Add curry leaves, green chilli, and turmeric. Mix quickly.
5. Add cooked rice and mix well.
6. Turn off flame. Squeeze lemon juice over rice. Mix gently.
7. Add salt and garnish with coriander. Serve.
Notes: Tastes great at room temperature. Perfect lunchbox recipe. Can add cashews for richness.

Recipe Name: Curd Rice
Meal Type: Lunch
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 10 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: cooked rice (2 cups), curd/yogurt (1 cup), milk (3 tbsp), mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilli (1), ginger (small piece), salt, oil, pomegranate or grapes (optional)
Steps:
1. Mash hot rice slightly with a spoon.
2. Add curd and milk. Mix well until creamy.
3. Add salt and mix.
4. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds.
5. Add curry leaves, green chilli, and ginger. Sauté for 30 seconds.
6. Pour this tempering over the curd rice. Mix.
7. Garnish with pomegranate or grapes if available. Serve.
Notes: Best served at room temperature or slightly chilled. Soothing and comforting meal.

Recipe Name: Aloo Paratha
Meal Type: Lunch
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 30 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
Spice Level: Medium
Ingredients: whole wheat flour (2 cups), potato (3 boiled), onion (1 small), green chilli (1), coriander, ajwain (1/2 tsp), salt, butter or ghee, cumin powder (1/2 tsp), amchur/dry mango powder (1/2 tsp)
Steps:
1. Knead flour with water and a pinch of salt to make soft dough. Rest for 15 minutes.
2. Mash boiled potatoes completely. Add chopped onion, chilli, coriander, ajwain, cumin powder, amchur, and salt. Mix well.
3. Divide dough and stuffing into equal portions.
4. Roll a dough ball flat. Place stuffing in center. Fold edges over stuffing and seal.
5. Gently roll again into a round paratha.
6. Cook on hot tawa with butter or ghee on both sides until golden.
7. Serve with curd, pickle, or chutney.
Notes: Make sure stuffing is dry and not wet, otherwise paratha may tear.
