Recipe Name: Vegetable Pulao
Meal Type: Dinner
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 30 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: basmati rice (1.5 cups), onion (1), carrot (1), peas (1/2 cup), potato (1), bay leaf (1), cloves (3), cardamom (2), cinnamon (1 stick), cumin seeds (1 tsp), ginger-garlic paste (1 tsp), mint leaves, salt, oil or ghee
Steps:
1. Wash and soak rice for 20 minutes. Drain.
2. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon.
3. Add cumin seeds. Let splutter.
4. Add onion and sauté until golden.
5. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute.
6. Add all vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
7. Add drained rice and mix gently.
8. Add 3 cups of water and salt. Add mint leaves.
9. Pressure cook for 2 whistles. Let pressure release. Serve hot with raita.
Notes: You can use a regular pot too — cover and cook for 15-18 minutes on low flame.

Recipe Name: Masala Maggi
Meal Type: Dinner
Cuisine: Indian
Cooking Time: 10 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Medium
Ingredients: Maggi noodles (2 packets), onion (1), tomato (1), capsicum (1/2), green chilli (1), garlic (2 cloves), egg (1 optional), Maggi masala (included), chilli flakes, oil, salt
Steps:
1. Boil 2 cups of water with a pinch of salt.
2. Heat oil in a separate pan. Add minced garlic.
3. Add chopped onion, green chilli, capsicum. Sauté for 2 minutes.
4. Add chopped tomato and cook until soft.
5. If adding egg, crack it in and scramble.
6. Add the boiled Maggi noodles and Maggi masala packet.
7. Add chilli flakes and mix well. Cook for 2 minutes.
8. Serve hot straight from the pan.
Notes: Add more veggies like corn or peas for a healthier version. Great quick dinner.

Recipe Name: Pasta
Meal Type: Dinner
Cuisine: Italian
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: pasta (2 cups), onion (1), tomato (2), garlic (4 cloves), capsicum (1), olive oil or butter, salt, pepper, red chilli flakes, oregano, mixed herbs, cheese (optional)
Steps:
1. Boil pasta in salted water until al dente (as per package time). Drain and keep aside.
2. Heat oil in a pan. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute.
3. Add chopped onion and cook until soft.
4. Add capsicum and sauté for 2 minutes.
5. Add chopped tomatoes. Cook until they break down into a sauce (5-7 minutes).
6. Add salt, pepper, red chilli flakes, oregano, and mixed herbs.
7. Add cooked pasta and toss everything together.
8. Top with cheese if desired. Serve hot.
Notes: Add a spoon of butter at the end for richness. Works with any pasta shape.

Recipe Name: Veg Noodles
Meal Type: Dinner
Cuisine: Chinese
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Medium
Ingredients: noodles (200g), onion (1), carrot (1), capsicum (1), cabbage (1 cup), garlic (3 cloves), soy sauce (2 tbsp), vinegar (1 tsp), pepper, salt, oil, spring onion
Steps:
1. Boil noodles in salted water until cooked. Drain and toss with a drop of oil.
2. Heat oil in a wok on high flame.
3. Add minced garlic. Sauté for 30 seconds.
4. Add onion, carrot, cabbage, capsicum. Stir fry on high heat for 3 minutes.
5. Add boiled noodles and toss everything together.
6. Add soy sauce, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Toss well.
7. Garnish with spring onion. Serve hot.
Notes: Keep flame high for stir fry texture. Don't overcook the vegetables.

Recipe Name: Tomato Soup
Meal Type: Dinner
Cuisine: Continental
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Spice Level: Mild
Ingredients: tomatoes (5 ripe), onion (1), garlic (3 cloves), butter, cream (2 tbsp optional), salt, pepper, sugar (1 tsp), oregano, bread (for croutons)
Steps:
1. Roughly chop tomatoes, onion, and garlic.
2. Boil tomatoes and onion with 1 cup water for 10 minutes until soft.
3. Let cool slightly. Blend until smooth.
4. Strain through a sieve if desired for smooth soup.
5. Heat butter in a pot. Add garlic and sauté.
6. Pour blended soup. Add salt, pepper, sugar, and oregano.
7. Simmer for 5 minutes. Add cream and mix.
8. Serve hot with croutons or bread.
Notes: The sugar balances the acidity of tomatoes. Add more cream for richness.
