INGREDIENT SUBSTITUTION GUIDE FOR HOME COOKS
=============================================

This file helps FoodieBot suggest alternatives when you're missing an ingredient.

---

COMMON SUBSTITUTES:

Ingredient: Egg
Substitutes:
- Mashed banana (for baking): 1 egg = 1/2 banana
- Flaxseed meal: 1 tbsp flaxseed + 3 tbsp water = 1 egg (let sit 5 mins)
- Curd/yogurt: 3 tbsp = 1 egg (for binding in bakes)
Notes: Eggs add binding and moisture. Choose substitute based on the recipe purpose.

---

Ingredient: Butter
Substitutes:
- Oil: Use 3/4 the amount of butter called for
- Ghee: 1:1 ratio, adds rich flavor
- Curd: For baking moisture (not for sautéing)
Notes: Ghee is best Indian substitute for butter in most recipes.

---

Ingredient: All-purpose flour / Maida
Substitutes:
- Whole wheat flour: 1:1, makes slightly denser result
- Rice flour: Good for crispy coatings
- Besan (gram flour): Adds nutty flavor, good for binding
Notes: For crispy coatings, rice flour works better than wheat flour.

---

Ingredient: Cream
Substitutes:
- Full fat milk: Reduces richness but works in soups
- Curd + milk mixture: 2 tbsp curd + 2 tbsp milk
- Coconut cream: Great for Indian curries
Notes: Add curd off the flame to prevent curdling.

---

Ingredient: Paneer
Substitutes:
- Tofu: Similar texture, less rich
- Boiled potato cubes: Absorbs spices well
- Boiled eggs: Different texture but works in rolls
Notes: Paneer is firmer than tofu. Press tofu dry before using as substitute.

---

Ingredient: Basmati Rice
Substitutes:
- Regular white rice: Works for most recipes
- Broken wheat/dalia: More nutritious
- Quinoa: For a healthier pulao
Notes: Basmati has a distinct aroma. For pulao, it makes a big difference.

---

Ingredient: Soy Sauce
Substitutes:
- Salt + a drop of vinegar: Basic substitute
- Coconut aminos: Slightly sweeter
- Worcestershire sauce: Adds depth (not vegan)
Notes: Soy sauce adds umami and color. Reduce salt elsewhere when using soy sauce.

---

Ingredient: Lemon / Lemon Juice
Substitutes:
- Amchur (dry mango powder): 1/2 tsp = juice of 1/2 lemon
- Tamarind water: Adds sourness with different flavor
- White vinegar: 1/2 the amount (stronger)
Notes: Amchur is the most common Indian substitute for lemon.

---

Ingredient: Green Chilli
Substitutes:
- Red chilli powder: 1/4 tsp per chilli
- Black pepper: Milder heat
- Jalapeño: Milder than green chilli
Notes: Fresh green chilli gives a different heat and flavor than dry chilli powder.

---

Ingredient: Onion
Substitutes:
- Spring onion / green onion: Milder flavor
- Shallots: Sweeter and smaller
- Onion powder: 1 tsp powder = 1 medium onion (only for cooked dishes)
Notes: Raw onion in salads is hard to substitute — shallots work best there.

---

Ingredient: Garlic
Substitutes:
- Garlic powder: 1/8 tsp = 1 clove
- Asafoetida/Hing: Small pinch, very different but adds depth
- Ginger: Won't taste the same but adds aroma
Notes: Fresh garlic is always best. Garlic powder is the easiest substitute.

---

Ingredient: Milk
Substitutes:
- Coconut milk: Rich, adds coconut flavor
- Soy milk: Neutral flavor
- Water: For thinning batters (less rich)
- Curd + water: For Indian recipes
Notes: For chai, coconut milk works well as a vegan alternative.

---

TIPS FOR SMART SUBSTITUTIONS:
1. Always consider the role of the ingredient: Is it for flavor, texture, or binding?
2. Adjust quantities carefully — some substitutes are stronger.
3. Taste as you cook and adjust.
4. Common ingredients like onion, garlic, potato, tomato, and rice are available year-round and are great base ingredients.
5. If you have eggs, bread, potato, and onion — you can make something tasty with almost any recipe in this dataset.
